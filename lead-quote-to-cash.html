Project: Lead/Quote to Cash Process Optimization

💢 **Problem:** The organization faced significant integration breakdowns within its end-to-end Lead-to-Cash process. This was primarily due to disparate legacy and NextGen systems, leading to data inconsistencies, manual workarounds, and delays in sales and revenue operations.

💡 **Solution:** My role involved driving the optimization and integration of this critical business process. Key activities included:

  • Conducting in-depth **requirements gathering** from various stakeholders across Sales, Finance, Operations, and IT to understand their needs and challenges within the Lead-to-Cash cycle.
  • Analyzing existing data sources and their structures to identify integration points and potential data discrepancies.
  • Creating detailed **data mapping diagrams** to illustrate how data would flow and transform between legacy and new systems.
  • Working closely with development teams to ensure **SQL scripts** were designed to clean, transform, and accurately migrate data to the new integrated system.
  • Supporting and overseeing **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)** to validate that the integrated process met business requirements and stakeholder expectations.
  • Implementing robust **defect tracking** and resolution processes to ensure high quality and smooth transitions.
  • Facilitating effective **stakeholder management** through regular communication and workshops to manage expectations and ensure project alignment.

📜 **Tools & Methodologies:** SQL, Python, Data Mapping, UAT tracking, Defects tracking, Stakeholder Management.

My Contribution & Impact

My primary contribution was bridging the gap between business needs and technical execution in this complex integration project. I ensured that every data point and process step in the Lead-to-Cash journey was accounted for, from initial lead capture to final revenue recognition. This involved a deep dive into both current state inefficiencies and desired future state capabilities, translating business objectives into technical specifications that guided development.
