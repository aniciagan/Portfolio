Approach to BRD & FRD Development

My process for developing robust requirements documents involves:

  • **Stakeholder Workshops:** Facilitating sessions with business users, product owners, and technical teams to elicit and clarify needs.
  • **Scope Definition:** Clearly defining what is in and out of scope for the project, establishing boundaries.
  • **Business Goals & Objectives:** Documenting the overarching business problems and the desired outcomes of the solution.
  • **Use Cases & Scenarios:** Describing how users will interact with the system to achieve specific goals, often including pre-conditions and post-conditions.
  • **Functional Requirements:** Detailing specific system behaviors, inputs, outputs, and data processing rules (e.g., "The system shall allow users to upload files up to 10MB in PDF format").
  • **Non-Functional Requirements:** Addressing aspects like performance, security, usability, and scalability.
  • **Data Requirements:** Specifying data entities, attributes, relationships, and data quality standards.
  • **User Interface (UI) Requirements:** Describing screen layouts, navigation, and user interactions (often supplemented with wireframes).
  • **Review Cycles:** Conducting iterative reviews with all relevant stakeholders to ensure accuracy, completeness, and consensus.
  • **Traceability Matrix:** Maintaining traceability between requirements, design, development, and testing to ensure all needs are met.

These documents serve as foundational blueprints, guiding development, informing testing strategies, and ensuring alignment across all project phases.
